Kakuzu Curiosities

1.

The first kanji in Kakuzu's name (角) stands for kakugyō, the bishop in shōgi. While similarly, the kanji in his partner Hidan's name means "rook", another shōgi piece.

2.

Kakuzu is the oldest shinobi whose age is known.

3.

Kakuzu's birthday corresponds with the astrological sign Leo, who rules the heart, the organ Kakuzu collected from defeated enemies.

4.

Kishimoto first intended to make Akatsuki's members monsters with almost no human traits. Zetsu, Kisame and Kakuzu (and, to a lesser extent, Deidara and Sasori) are prime examples of this. Kakuzu, in particular, has traits derived from a Kuchisake-onna (口裂け女, literally meaning: Slit-Mouthed Woman), a Japanese mythological figure who has a slit across her mouth from ear to ear (as with Kakuzu's), which is hidden under a surgical mask (as Kakuzu also hid his mouth under a similar mask).

5.

Many of Kakuzu's techniques are named after various mecha from Mobile Suit Gundam (機動戦士ガンダム, Kidō Senshi Gandamu). In Chapter 60, Kishimoto mentioned his interest in the show in the third segment of his child history. He used to draw Zaku, Gifu (Gouf), Domu (Dom), Jimu (GM), and Dozuruzabi (Dozle Zabi).

6.

Although covered by his hood, Kakuzu had shoulder length dark brown hair, but in the anime, it was depicted reaching down to his lower back.

7.

When Kakuzu first appeared as a hologram at the end of Part I, he was clearly the tallest Akatsuki member there, a fact reinforced when he appeared during Gaara's sealing ritual.[27] However, when he truly appeared with Hidan, he was much shorter, and the third databook stated that he was shorter than Kisame.

8.

Kakuzu's unusually coloured eyes were similar to those of Hanzō.

9.

Both Hidan and Kakuzu's faces appeared on a pair of lanterns in the omake of Shippūden episode 129, Kakuzu being on the right and Hidan on the left.

10.

Kakuzu, along with Hidan, gets a mention in Naruto Shippūden: Dragon Blade Chronicles for the Wii, when Tobi mentions "monsters" with traits of the two (arms that can be stretched and the ability to survive decapitation), before Deidara notes how he is talking about the pair's abilities.

11.

The markings on Kakuzu's forearms suggest that he has been in prison at some point, as such tattoos were used during Edo period in Japan to identify criminals according to the institution they were in.

12.

Kakuzu's favourite food was liver sashimi and monkfish liver, while his least was Chestnut jelly.

13.

He wished to fight anyone with a high bounty.

14.

His hobby was reading (old/rare books).

15.

Kakuzu's favourite phrase was "If you're prepared, there won't be any sorrow" (備えあれば憂いなし, Sonae areba urei nashi).
